Tic-Tac-Toe: Expert Rules Challenge

Play three games against each other. Every move you make is checked against the same rules an expert tic-tac-toe player follows — see how many you match.

How this works

  1. Two players share this device and take turns clicking a square.
  2. After each move, you'll see whether it matched the expert rule for that situation: +1 if it did, −1 if it didn't.
  3. At the end of each game: +1 for a win, 0 for a draw, −1 for a loss.
  4. You'll play 3 games. Whoever goes first alternates each game. Your final score is the total across all 3 games.

Expert rule hierarchy (Crowley & Siegler, 1993)

Checked in this order — the first rule that applies defines the expert move for that turn.

  1. Win — complete a line if you can.
  2. Block — stop your opponent from completing a line.
  3. Fork — create two winning threats at once.
  4. Block Fork — stop your opponent from creating a fork (either by forcing them to block you instead, or by occupying the fork square directly).
  5. Play Center — take the middle square if it's open.
  6. Play Opposite Corner — if your opponent is in a corner, take the opposite one.
  7. Play Empty Corner — take any open corner.
  8. Play Empty Side — take any open edge square.
